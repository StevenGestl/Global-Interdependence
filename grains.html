Global Interdependence and Grains

Global grain trade has increased considerably since the early days of trading. Today an estimated 576 million tons of grain are traded each year. The United States has a large presence in the grain trade of the world.

Surprisingly, Russia is the largest wheat exporting in the world, a nearly $8 billion industry. Additionally, Ukraine is the 5th largest exporter of wheat with a $3.5 Billion industry. Sanctions and the ongoing war in the Ukraine will likely change this in the future.

Throughout history war and political issues have drastically altered trade partnerships. In the 1970s Strict sanctions on U.S. grain forced Japan to move to another country to help them meet their needs. At the time Brazil had a small grain industry that would go on to be a massive force.

The United States will import over 6 million tons of grain from other countries this year alone. Nearly $500 million will be wheat from countries like Russia, Canada, and France.

(Grain Exports, USDA)
