Is This Produce Local?

The average shopper might be surprised how much of their vegetables are imported.

Global Interdependence and Vegetables

Walk into any produce section in the United States and youâ€™ll be visited by a diverse array of vegetables both native and foreign. Over one third of vegetables consumed in the United States are imported from another country. This is good for consumers as it offers greater variety and availability of their favorite vegetables.

The United States biggest export partners are Canada, Mexico, South Korea, and Japan. These export partners generated $7.2 billion for the United States in 2021(Fruits and Vegetables).

While exporting all this produce, the United States also imports plenty of produce, over $15 billion worth. This imported produce comes mostly from Canada, Mexico, Peru, Guatemala, and Costa Rica.

It is estimated that in the coming decade the United States need for imports will continue to increase at a rapid rate. Importing and exporting with other countries increases quality of life for all participants through global interdependence.
