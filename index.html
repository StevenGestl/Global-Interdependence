What is Global Interdependence?

Global interdependence means mutual dependence at a global level. Wherein, a country depends on the other for something and that country further depends on another country, this leads to a situation of global interdependence.

Global Interdependence and Food Supply

Global food supply is a complex and diverse system. Many countries participate in importing and exporting goods to make up the global interdependence that feeds billions of people daily.

Countries need for importing and exporting depends greatly on their nation's geography. Countries with large amounts of land for agriculture will produce more produce than countries with poor agricultural conditions. Similarly, countries with ocean access will supply more fish than land locked nations.

Today only a few countries make up the majority of agricultural exports. The United States exports many goods to other nations while importing goods for their own nation. While the United States has a large export presence, they aren’t the leader in exports internationally.

Many of the goods produced in the United States are shipped abroad to other nations. For example, nearly 75% of the tree nuts that are produced in the United States are exported. The United States also has a large import of goods that can’t be produced within the country. For example, The United States imports nearly 100% of all coffee that is consumed.

(Investopedia, Ross)
