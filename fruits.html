Global Interdependence and Fruits

Similar to fruit consumers are offered a wide variety of vegetables both native and foreign. The United States exports roughly $5 billion in fruits every year, 2022 is proving to be a record year.

The United States imports nearly double the amount of fruit that is exported, $15 Billion worth. Top imports include berries, avocados, and bananas.

The main fruit exporters of the world in order are the United States, Spain, the Netherlands, Mexico, and China. There are only two nations that sell more than $10 billion worth of fruit, they are the United States and Spain.

As populations increase around the world there will be a higher demand for all food supply imports. These imports are especially critical in poorer nations that will heavily rely on imports from other more well-off nations.

(The Packer, Karst)
