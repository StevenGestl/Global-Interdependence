Global Interdependence and Proteins

In the United States imported proteins are rarer than the other food groups. Only about 10-20% of U.S. meat supplies are imported, this includes prepackaged and livestock. Generally, the meat that is imported from other countries are from Canada or Mexico. The quantity of these imports will change based on livestock conditions in the United States.

In the United States approximately 90% of all meat this is processed are from chickens. This isn’t due some increase in chicken, it’s caused by the small size of chickens when compared to a cow for instance.

As of October 2022, the United States has exported 6% more beef than 2021, these imports have been shipped to over 100 countries. Japan, South Korea, and China are the biggest customers in these imports. The United States is seeing gains in market share due to a drought in Australia that disrupted their exports.

Like many global supply chains there are many outside factors that can disrupt meat production, such as drought, hurricanes, tornadoes, and fires.
